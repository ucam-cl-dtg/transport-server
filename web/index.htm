OmniBus.

Britain's Public Transport Data made accessible.

For developers

The United Kingdom's public transport data is dispersed and difficult to access. OmniBus aims to be a central access point for all of Britain's public transport data.

Using OmniBus your can develop applications that take advantage of public transport data.

For transport operators

Increase the accessibility of your public transport. Making your data available through OmniBus allows local residents to access information more easily on the move. For example, the miniBus application for the Android mobile phone allows users to retrieve real-time bus arrival information.

If you are interested in working with us we'd love to hear from you—please e-mail dtg-android@cl.cam.ac.uk.

Starting using OmniBus

To start using OmniBus, you will need an API key.

Documentation

Once you have a developer key, head to our developer guide to learn how to query and interpret responses from OmniBus.

Coverage

OmniBus currently covers every bus stop in Cambridgeshire.

Who runs OmniBus

OmniBus is the product of a summer research project by David Tattersall, a student at the University of Cambridge Computer Laboratory. The Computer Laboratory continue to run OmniBus and we're working hard at bringing more data providers to the service.

Thanks to Alastair Beresford, Andy Rice and all participating councils and transport operators.
